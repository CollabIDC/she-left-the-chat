She Made Sure I Wasn't Alone at Midnight

She Actually Did It

By Kimberly  ·  January 2026  ·  6 min read

I am not someone who shows up to a holiday without a plan. I am also not someone who shows up without a backup plan. Christmas Eve in Madrid humbled both of them.

So by New Year's Eve, I was ready. Chicken Caesar salad, ribs, wings, mango margarita, and dessert on the table. Enough food for a few days, honestly, but that felt right. A bouquet of tulips my relocation company had sent as a welcome gift was still sitting on the table, and I had not moved them. They made the whole space feel like someone was glad I was there.

I was dressed. I had my wine glass. And I had a scheduled FaceTime with my best friend at 5:45 her time, which was 11:45 mine.

What I did not know was that she would stay.

She had somewhere to be that night. An actual New Year's Eve event. She was dressed too, wine glass ready, on her own timeline. But she carved out that call before she walked out her door, and then she just... did not leave. For the next hour or so, we hunted through YouTube together looking for celebrations because the Madrid live stream turned out to be mostly a camera pointed at a crowd. So we watched other countries ring in their midnights instead. Different cities, different countdowns, all of it loud and joyful in ways we could share from six hours apart.

We hung up somewhere around 1 AM my time.

I had not spent New Year's Eve alone. Not even close.

That is the thing nobody really tells you about making a bold life change. Everyone talks about the courage it takes to go. Not enough people talk about the ones who quietly make sure you are held while you figure out how to stay. My best friend did not move to Madrid. But she showed up for midnight anyway, dressed for her own celebration, making time for mine.

The tulips were already on the table. The food was already ordered. But she is the reason that night felt like something worth remembering.

Spain has not changed. I am the one adapting. And apparently, so is my best friend, one time zone at a time.
